🐙 Fact 1: The Amazing Octopus

Octopuses have three hearts, nine brains, and blue blood. Two hearts pump blood to the gills, while the third pumps blood to the rest of the body.

How smart are octopuses?

Octopuses can open jars, solve puzzles, and even recognize human faces. Scientists consider them one of the most intelligent invertebrates on Earth.

⚡Fact 2: Lightning Strikes

Lightning strikes the Earth about 100 times every second. That's roughly 8 million lightning strikes per day!

How hot is lightning?

A single bolt of lightning is around 30,000 Kelvin - five times hotter than the surface of the Sun.

Can lightning strike the same place twice?

Yes! lightning absolutely can and does strike the same place more than once, especially tall structures like towers and trees.

🌊 Fact 3: The Deep Ocean

More than 80% of the world's oceans remain unexplored. Scientists believe that there are millions of species living in the deep sea that we have never discovered

How deep does the ocean go?

The deepest point on earth is the Mariana Trench, which reaches a depth of about 11 kilometers. - deep enough to fit Mount Everest inside with room to spare!

Fun Comparison

If you placed Mount Everest at the bottom of the Mariana Trench, its peak would still be more than 2 kilometers underwater.
